# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Members of a season-long PGA Tour pick 'em league, a mix of casual friends-and-family fans and dedicated golf followers who track the Tour closely. Most visits happen on a phone, often during tournament week, sometimes outdoors in sunlight, frequently mid-conversation with other members. Two recurring contexts dominate: deciding and locking this week's golfer before the Thursday deadline, and checking where they stand against rivals. A single admin runs the league (tournaments, payments, penalties) from the same app.

## Product Purpose

Run a private, season-long fantasy game where each member picks one golfer per tournament and scores the actual prize money that golfer earns, with every golfer usable only once per season. The app exists to make the weekly ritual (pick, watch, settle up) trustworthy and effortless: clear deadlines, an unambiguous picture of who is available, live and final earnings, accurate standings, and a side-pot economy (entry fees, major missed-cut penalties) that members trust without having to audit. Success is members reaching for it every tournament week without friction or doubt about the numbers.

## Brand Personality

Classic, sporting, trusted. The voice is that of a well-run clubhouse: confident, warm, a little ceremonial, never hyped. It speaks to people who respect the game. Earnings and standings are presented with the gravity of a ledger, not the adrenaline of a betting slate. Restraint signals seriousness; the personality supports the game rather than competing with it.

## Anti-references

- **Generic SaaS dashboard.** No cream-and-blue startup template, no hero-metric tiles, no identical icon-and-heading card grids, nothing that reads as "an AI made this." The product has a specific heritage-golf identity and should never collapse into interchangeable dashboard chrome.
- **Corporate enterprise tool.** No cold, dense, gray admin-panel feel. Even the admin surfaces carry the clubhouse warmth; density never wins over legibility and character.
- Adjacent to avoid: sportsbook loudness (neon odds, flashing promos, gamble-y urgency) runs counter to "classic, sporting, trusted" and should be kept out by reflex.

## Design Principles

- **Points are real dollars, so show them like a ledger.** Earnings and standings carry literal money stakes. Numbers get the clarity and gravity of a financial statement: aligned, precise, never decorative.
- **Clubhouse, not sportsbook.** Heritage golf restraint and warmth over neon urgency and promo hype. Earn engagement through trust, not adrenaline.
- **Glanceable first.** The primary device is a phone, often in sunlight, often mid-banter. The one number that matters on any screen (your rank, your active pick, the deadline) reads in a single glance.
- **Two equal front doors.** Making this week's pick and checking the standings are both first-class jobs. The design never makes one subordinate to the other.
- **Subtle rules demand obvious state.** Backup activation, withdrawal handling, major 1.5x multipliers, missed-cut penalties: members only trust the game when pick status and earnings are unambiguous at every step. When the rule is complex, the display must be plain.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.1 AA. Specific commitments driven by the audience and context: AA contrast across the green/gold palette (including earnings badges and status pills), comfortable body type sizing for a range of ages, large tap targets for thumb use on phones, and legibility in bright outdoor light. Respect reduced-motion preferences for any added animation. No known assistive-tech requirements beyond standard semantic HTML, focus states, and labeled controls.
